Group Reflection

Justin Healy

What our group has done well so far has been our ability to stick to our deadlines. During our meetings we have specified which work was needed to be completed and when. So far, we have all stuck to these deadlines and have been able to offer our pieces of work to the group for absorption into the collective.

What could be improved in our team, is our ability to schedule meetings. Most of our meetings have been organised the day of, or the day before. We could improve by having a more structured meeting system.

The thing that has surprised me about this experience is the same thing I have I learnt. It is that it takes time to settle into a groove with a group, even when all communication is online. I expected this part to be much easier as an online experience, but it is essentially how it would be if we were physically in a classroom together. We all had to find our way of contributing our parts and find our place in the team.

In the way we structured GitHub, we used teams to host and edit all our sections, then when completed, these could be pushed to GitHub for web hosting.

Another aspect I think our team did very well is that we split the sections among each member in the start very carefully before we started doing anything. Marc Valpiani

What went well

The group dynamic was very friendly and team members completed tasks on time at a good standard. Meetings were quite productive and didnâ€™t drag on unnecessarily.

What could be improved

We faced a few challenges with group formation and settling in to the assignment. One of our original group members ended up not responding and we had some difficulty assigning tasks as we did not know how many people we should split our work between. This was somewhat out of our control though, but it might have been a good idea to confirm at the very beginning whether group members were planning to stay enrolled in the course.

The main thing that could have been improved is planning meetings further in advance. We had to cancel a few meetings at the last minute because we could not get everyone online at the same time. I do not think this was anyone's fault personally, more a result of a group with different schedules trying to collaborate on a project.

One thing I learned about groups

While this is fairly self evident, this assignment reinforced the idea that people have vastly different interests, strengths and skill sets. For example, when dividing the workload in our group it was interesting to note that each group member had different ideas about which sections that they wanted to work on. For example, one of our group members has very strong presentation skills, an area where I lack interest and skill, whereas I enjoy researching and writing and was inclined to work on the technology reports.

Before I start, it is prudent that you know I have worked in a variety of Programming Development teams in the past.

Overall the group proceeded well into the assignment straight away. I think, this particular group of individuals were already equiped with an intrinsic will to succeed. Although, I guess that is already a given expectation, being that we have all enrolled in University for a specific purpose.

Personally, I have enjoyed the experience. Interactions with such individuals has provided me with a little extra drive. The expectations are high, given the skill sets of each person. After sharing our, 'Assignment One', marks I knew I was in for a hard time if we were ever to compete. This, reality check, for each person forced output worthy of University level expectations.

Having said all of the above, from experience, I know that when a team operates in such an efficient manner there are usually some hidden, under the surface problems, that we have not yet addressed.

I say this only, for I think there were a few operational processes that we need to work on. They all stem around communication. I myself am not isolated from this peice of constructive criticism either.

Above all, I think we performed well together, which has me a little worried if things do not work well in the future.

Groups don't don't always get along. There are occasions when we all become agitated or annoyed with others, I find when giving yourself a little time away comes understanding. Some of the perceived big issues are not a concern. So, in a couple of words patience comes understanding.

MS Teams


This was the area where the majority of our communication was conducted. The chatting area in particular. Our group meetings were also conducted through this environment.

At first, it was a little slow to begin with, but once everybody developed a competent understanding of the platform, its utilization subsequently increased.

It should be understood, that not everybody is contactible and easily accessible owing to other commitments (work), but overall everybody got in the habit of regularly checking it and contributing and answering questions when asked.

All of us attempted to use the 'Files' section well. Although, the level of documentation needs to increase. A review of this process, in particular the 'check in' and 'check out' procedures. As nobody did this there is potential for conflict here.

A worksplit document was established at the very beginning. However, it was not always referred to, myself included. The document needs to be divided up into the relevant sections and then reamalgameted towards the end.

The bulk of the contend was not shared until the very end. Progress needs to be shown and committed to the repository on a daily basis if and when possible. This would enable a clearer and better understanding of everyones progress. Above all, it would promote a peer review system. Although, this did not seem to hinder us from acheiving a pleasing result.
