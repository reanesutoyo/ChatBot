GROUP PROCESS

One of the most beneficial aspects of working in a team was the diverse perspectives that everyone had on the assignment. Although there were some disagreements, there were some excellent questions that prompted me to consider subjects I had not considered previously. Although we were all working remotely, we were constantly communicating via messages and video conferencing in Microsoft Teams, which demonstrated our group's ability to work collaboratively and resolve issues.

However, what made working in a team environment in a university setting unique was the lack of leadership. Unlike real-world projects, where there is always a team leader or manager leading the team, there was no set leader for us, and as a result of the lack of leadership, there was sense in the lack of unity and structure in completing the assignment.

Overall, the experience was positive and demonstrated to me how closely teamwork, leadership, time management, and productivity are intertwined, and how a deficiency in any of these areas would have consequences. Individuals are most productive when they manage their time wisely, and teamwork is most effective and effortless when all members work cooperatively toward the same goals.
